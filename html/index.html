Meet the Coaches

Experienced swimmers. Friendly coaching.

Alex Daile

Alex is an incoming Division I swimmer at the University of Pennsylvania and a Toyota U.S. National Championships Qualifier. Ranked as a Top 100 recruit nationally and the #1 recruit in Massachusetts, Alex has competed at some of the highest levels of age-group and high school swimming in the country. As a coach, Alex specializes in stroke technique, race development, starts, turns, and helping swimmers build efficient habits that lead to long-term improvement. Whether working with a beginner learning the fundamentals or a competitive swimmer chasing personal bests, Alex focuses on creating a positive, goal-oriented environment where swimmers can develop confidence and reach their full potential.

Brady Kam

Brady is a Division I swimmer at American University, a Goldfish Swim School instructor, and a Futures Championship qualifier with national-level competitive swimming experience. Through both coaching and competition, Brady has developed a strong understanding of how to help swimmers improve their skills while building confidence in the water. Brady has experience teaching swimmers of all ages and ability levels, from young beginners learning water safety to athletes refining technique for competition. His patient, encouraging approach helps swimmers feel comfortable, motivated, and excited to improve with every lesson.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do we need our own pool?

Yes. We travel to your pool, including backyard, condo, apartment, or community pools where you have access.

What ages do you teach?

We teach swimmers of all ages and ability levels.

Do you coach competitive swimmers?

Yes. We work on technique, race strategy, starts, turns, underwaters, and meet preparation.

Do you offer group lessons?

Yes. We offer private, semi-private, and small group lessons.

What should swimmers bring?

Swimsuit, goggles, towel, water bottle, and any equipment they already use.
